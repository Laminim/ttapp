Acoustic Coordinated Reset Neuromodulation
Find your frequency
–

Acoustic Coordinated Reset Neuromodulation (ACRN) is a tinnitus therapy that uses several short tones placed around a person's tinnitus frequency.

First the tinnitus pitch is measured. Then four tones slightly above and below that frequency are played in a repeating sequence through headphones.

The tones are brief and presented in a specific pattern. The goal is to disrupt abnormal synchronized activity in auditory neurons that is believed to contribute to tinnitus.

Over repeated sessions the brain may reduce this hyper-synchronization. Some patients report a decrease in tinnitus loudness over time.

The method was developed by researchers around Peter A. Tass at the Forschungszentrum Jülich, Germany.
